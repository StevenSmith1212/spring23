FOLDOC Definition

  1. OS - Low-level software that communicates with hardware, organizes tasks and storage, and presents a default interface.
  1. Automata whose distribution is transparent to the user so that the system appears as one local machine.
    1. Opposed to a network were it is known that there are multiple machines and locations
  1. Dist. Systems use a client-server organization

Textbook Definition

  1. collection of independent computers that appear to the suers of the system as a single computer - Andrew Tanenbaum
  1. Several computers doing something together. Characteristics include: Mulitple computers, interconnections, and shared states.

Working Definition for us

  1. collection of entities each of which is autonomous, programmable, asynchronous and failure-prone, and which communicate through an unreliable communication medium
  1. Entity = a process on a device
  1. communication medium = wired or wireless network
  1. our interest in distributed systems involves
    1. design and implementation, maintenance, algorithms.

Computers in a Dist. System

  • Composed of three types:
    • Workstations:
      • Computers used by end-users to perform computing
    • Server Machines:
      • Computers which provide resources and services
    • Personal Assistance Devices:
      • Handheld computers connected to the system via a wireless communication link

Centralized vs Dist. Computing

  • Centralized:
    • Computers are connected through mainframe computer
  • Distributed:
    • Computers are connected through a network link to other independent computers.
  • This difference allows for individual computing. With centralized computing the computers in the system are dependent on the mainframe

Why Distributed Computing?

  1. Economics: allow pooling of resources, including CPU cycles, data storage, input/output devices and services
  1. Reliability: allows replication of resources and/or services, thus reducing service outage due to failure
  1. possibility: for applications with physically distributed components

Advantages

  1. Affordable
  1. resource sharing
  1. scalability
  1. fault tolerance

Disadvantages

  1. Multiple Points of Failures: A single failure on a computer can end in trouble for the whole system
  1. Security Concerns: More opportunities for unauthorized attack

Heterogeneity

  1. Components must be able to interoperate:
    1. operating systems
    1. hardware architectures
    1. communication architectures
    1. programming languages
    1. software interfaces
    1. security measures
    1. information representation

Distribution Transparency

  1. Dist. should be hidden from users
    1. This gives the image that is a whole system rather than a collection
  1. Access Transparency
    1. connection to local or remote resources is identical
  1. location transparency
    1. access without knowledge of location
  1. Failure Transparency
    1. tasks can be completed despite failures
  1. Replication transparency
    1. replicated resources are treated as the same as the original. This gives reliability and performance
  1. Migration Transparency
    1. allow the movement of resources.
